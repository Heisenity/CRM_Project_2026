# Employee Attendance

**Feature name:** Employee Attendance  
**Route:** `/employee-attendance`

## What this feature does
Employee Attendance helps you record your workday in a simple way.

Main actions include:
- Clock in when your workday starts.
- Clock out when your workday ends.
- Capture a photo using your camera during attendance.
- Check your current attendance status.
- View task-related attendance actions (if shown on your account).

## When users should use it
Use this page:
- At the start of your shift.
- At the end of your shift.
- Any time you need to confirm whether your attendance was recorded.

## Step-by-step instructions
1. Open the app and log in as staff. The Staff Portal opens, and on the first page you can see **Employee Attendance**.
2. Open the **Daily Clock-in/out** section.
3. Allow camera access if the app asks for permission.
4. At the beginning of work, click **Clock In**.
5. Capture your photo when prompted and submit.
6. Wait for confirmation that your clock-in was recorded.
7. At the end of work, return to the same page and click **Clock Out**.
8. Capture your photo again if prompted, then submit.
9. Check your status on the page to confirm both actions are saved.

## Expected result
Your attendance for the day is recorded correctly and can be reviewed by your manager/admin.

Typical outcomes:
- Start time is captured when you clock in.
- End time is captured when you clock out.
- Attendance photos are attached to your clock-in/clock-out records.
- Your daily status appears correctly on the page.

## Common mistakes or errors
- Clock-in button not working: Check your internet connection and try again.
- Camera does not open: Allow camera permission in browser/device settings, then reload the page.
- Photo capture failed: Ensure your face is visible and there is enough light, then retake.
- Clock-out missed: Open the page again and clock out before leaving.
- Wrong account used: Sign out and log in with your own employee account.
- Status not updated: Refresh the page and wait a few seconds.
